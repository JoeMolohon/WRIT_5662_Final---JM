Digital Literacy Milestones

  1. 1999: My first experience with a computer. I began using computers when I was very young, primarily for education and games, which were usually intertwined.
  2. 2000-2004: Continued classes in computer literacy at school; mostly through simple classes focused on encouraging student familiarity with computers.
  3. 2005: My first computer typing course. This was when I was introduced to Microsoft Word, Excel, and PowerPoint.
  4. 2008: The purchase of my first laptop. This kickstarted more individualized learning for me, such as the recreational of email and word processors.
  5. 2014: My introduction to Photoshop. I used the software for some assignments, but my primary purpose was recreational.
  6. 2015: My introduction to HTML/CSS through a college course on web design principles. The year I created my first website.
  7. 2015: The year I began my role as editor of the 2016 issue of the Summit Avenue Review. Assisted in creation of literary magazine through Adobe InDesign and Illustrator.
  8. 2015: Hired as newsletter writer for World Jiu Jitsu Academy. Wrote newsletters through a combination of Word and Photoshop.
  9. 2016: Picked up Adobe Premiere through new Film Studies minor. Through minor, learned to edit and shoot video.
  10. 2017: Enrolled in additional Lynda courses on InDesign and FrameMaker.
  11. 2018: Introduction to Visio through work at Sitesafe. Further professional use of Excel and Word.
  12. 2019: Began present work at 3M. Primary use of InDesign and Illustrator; picked up AutoCAD and IsoDraw, additional 3D software literacy.
  13. 2019-???: Use of DITA and XML for development of a digital CMS for 3M Fall Protection. This is a project that is currently underway and that is driving development of my digital literacy to its next level.
